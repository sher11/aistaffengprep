# Behavioral Rounds - Question Bank

## Category 1: Technical Leadership

### Questions

**1.1**: "Tell me about a time you made a significant technical decision that affected your team or organization."

**Answer Framework**:
- Situation: What was the technical context?
- Task: What decision needed to be made?
- Action: How did you evaluate options, gather input, make the decision?
- Result: What was the outcome? What did you learn?

**Staff-level signals**:
- Considered long-term implications
- Sought diverse input
- Communicated decision clearly
- Owned the outcome

---

**1.2**: "Describe a time you improved engineering practices or code quality on your team."

**Answer Framework**:
- Identify specific practice/problem
- How you diagnosed the issue
- Steps to implement improvement
- Metrics showing impact

**Good Example Elements**:
- Introduced code review standards
- Set up automated testing
- Created documentation practices
- Mentored team on best practices

---

**1.3**: "Tell me about mentoring a junior engineer."

**Answer Framework**:
- Who you mentored and context
- Your mentoring approach
- Specific examples of guidance
- Their growth and outcomes

**Staff-level signals**:
- Tailored approach to individual
- Balanced teaching with enabling autonomy
- Celebrated their wins
- Long-term investment in their growth

---

**1.4**: "Describe a time you had to learn a new technology quickly to deliver a project."

**Answer Framework**:
- Why the new technology was needed
- Your learning approach
- How you applied it
- Results and lessons

---

## Category 2: Conflict & Collaboration

### Questions

**2.1**: "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a technical approach your team was taking."

**Answer Framework**:
- What was the disagreement?
- How did you voice your concerns?
- How was it resolved?
- What was the outcome and learning?

**Key Points**:
- Focused on the issue, not the person
- Provided data/evidence
- Remained open to being wrong
- Supported final decision regardless

---

**2.2**: "Describe working with a difficult stakeholder or colleague."

**Answer Framework**:
- Who and why difficult?
- Your approach to the relationship
- Specific actions taken
- Outcome and lessons

**Staff-level approach**:
- Sought to understand their perspective
- Found common ground
- Adjusted communication style
- Built productive relationship

---

**2.3**: "Tell me about a time you had to give difficult feedback."

**Answer Framework**:
- Context and why feedback was needed
- How you prepared
- How you delivered it
- Outcome and follow-up

**Good elements**:
- Timely delivery
- Specific and actionable
- Balanced positive and constructive
- Followed up on progress

---

**2.4**: "Describe a situation where you had to push back on a request from leadership."

**Answer Framework**:
- What was the request?
- Why did you disagree?
- How did you communicate your concerns?
- What was the resolution?

---

## Category 3: Failure & Growth

### Questions

**3.1**: "Tell me about a project that failed or didn't meet expectations."

**Answer Framework**:
- What was the project and goal?
- What went wrong?
- Your role in the failure
- What you learned and changed

**Staff-level response**:
- Takes appropriate ownership
- Shows deep reflection
- Demonstrates applied learnings
- No blame shifting

---

**3.2**: "Describe your biggest professional mistake."

**Answer Framework**:
- What was the mistake?
- Impact on team/project/company
- How you addressed it
- Long-term changes made

**Good mistakes to discuss**:
- Technical decisions with unexpected consequences
- Communication failures
- Delayed escalation of issues
- Underestimating complexity

---

**3.3**: "What's the toughest feedback you've received? How did you respond?"

**Answer Framework**:
- What was the feedback?
- Initial reaction (be honest)
- How you processed it
- Actions taken and results

---

**3.4**: "Tell me about a time you failed to meet a deadline."

**Answer Framework**:
- What was the deadline and why missed?
- How did you communicate?
- What did you do to mitigate?
- Prevention measures implemented

---

## Category 4: Influence & Leadership

### Questions

**4.1**: "Tell me about a time you influenced a decision without having direct authority."

**Answer Framework**:
- What decision and stakeholders?
- Your approach to influence
- How you built alignment
- Outcome achieved

**Influence tactics**:
- Data and evidence
- Building relationships
- Understanding motivations
- Finding win-win solutions

---

**4.2**: "Describe leading a cross-team or cross-functional initiative."

**Answer Framework**:
- What was the initiative?
- Challenges of cross-team work
- How you drove alignment
- Results and lessons

**Staff-level signals**:
- Aligned diverse stakeholders
- Managed without authority
- Navigated organizational complexity
- Delivered meaningful outcomes

---

**4.3**: "Tell me about a time you changed someone's mind on a technical approach."

**Answer Framework**:
- What was their position?
- Why did you disagree?
- How did you persuade them?
- Outcome and relationship impact

---

**4.4**: "Describe a time you had to rally a team through a difficult period."

**Answer Framework**:
- What was the difficult situation?
- How did you assess team morale?
- Specific actions taken
- Results and team feedback

---

## Category 5: Delivery & Execution

### Questions

**5.1**: "Tell me about delivering a project under significant pressure or tight deadline."

**Answer Framework**:
- What was the project and pressure?
- How did you prioritize?
- Trade-offs made
- Results achieved

---

**5.2**: "Describe managing a complex, ambiguous project."

**Answer Framework**:
- What made it complex/ambiguous?
- How did you create structure?
- How did you manage risk?
- Outcomes and lessons

**Staff-level approach**:
- Broke down into manageable pieces
- Identified and mitigated risks early
- Communicated progress and blockers
- Adapted as requirements evolved

---

**5.3**: "Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information."

**Answer Framework**:
- What was the decision?
- What information was missing?
- How did you proceed?
- Outcome and validation

---

**5.4**: "Describe unblocking a stuck project or team."

**Answer Framework**:
- What was stuck and why?
- How did you diagnose?
- Actions to unblock
- Results and prevention

---

## Category 6: Growth & Self-Awareness

### Questions

**6.1**: "What are your areas for growth?"

**Answer Framework**:
- Be honest and specific
- Show self-awareness
- Describe what you're doing about it
- Show progress

**Good areas to mention**:
- Specific technical skills
- Leadership/communication aspects
- Delegation/letting go
- Work-life balance

---

**6.2**: "How do you stay current technically?"

**Answer Framework**:
- Specific practices (reading, courses, projects)
- How you apply learnings
- Recent example

---

**6.3**: "Describe a time you received feedback that surprised you."

**Answer Framework**:
- What was the surprise?
- Why didn't you see it?
- How did you process it?
- Changes made

---

**6.4**: "What would your team say about working with you?"

**Answer Framework**:
- Positive attributes (with evidence)
- Areas they might mention for growth
- Shows you've sought feedback

---
