Module 6: Influence Without Authority

Learning Objectives

  • Demonstrate staff-level influence patterns
  • Show cross-team alignment abilities
  • Navigate organizational complexity
  • Lead without formal authority

Why Influence Matters at Staff Level

Staff engineers must:

  • Drive technical initiatives across teams
  • Change entrenched behaviors/practices
  • Align stakeholders with different incentives
  • Lead without formal reporting structure

Common Questions

"How have you influenced without authority?"

Focus on: understanding stakeholders, building relationships, data-driven persuasion

"Tell me about leading a cross-team initiative"

Focus on: alignment challenges, coalition building, delivering results

"Describe changing someone's mind"

Focus on: understanding their position, finding common ground, win-win outcome

"Tell me about rallying a team through difficulty"

Focus on: assessing morale, specific actions, team feedback

Staff-Level Signals

Phrases That Show Influence Skills ▼
  • "I didn't have authority, so I..."
  • "I scheduled 1:1s with key stakeholders to understand..."
  • "I presented data showing..."
  • "By finding common ground, I was able to..."
  • "I built a coalition with Teams A and B first..."
  • "I incorporated their concerns into my proposal..."

Example Response

Strong Influence Story

Situation: "We needed to adopt a new testing framework across three teams, but each team lead had their own preferred approach. I didn't have authority to mandate a choice."

Building Influence: "I scheduled 1:1s with each lead to understand their priorities. Team A valued speed, Team B valued reliability, Team C worried about migration cost. I created a comparison matrix showing how my recommendation addressed each concern. I found that Teams A and C had similar underlying needs, so I aligned them first."

Resolution: "With two of three teams on board, I scheduled a joint meeting. Team B raised valid edge case concerns, which I incorporated into the adoption plan. We agreed on a phased rollout."

Outcome: "Six months later, all three teams use the framework. Test coverage increased 40%. The relationships I built have made future cross-team work easier."

Anti-Patterns

Using Authority You Don't Have

"I told them we were doing it my way" shows poor influence skills.

Escalating First

Running to management before trying to build alignment.

Ignoring Concerns

Bulldozing over valid objections damages relationships.

Win-Lose Outcomes

Good influence finds solutions that work for everyone.
