Module 2: Story Mining & Preparation

Learning Objectives

  • Systematically mine experiences for interview stories
  • Create a categorized story matrix
  • Write and refine 10-15 polished stories
  • Adapt stories for different question types

Story Mining Process

flowchart TD A[All Career Experiences] --> B[Filter: Last 3-5 Years] B --> C[Identify: 30+ Potential Stories] C --> D[Develop: 15-20 Drafted Stories] D --> E[Polish: 10-15 Final Stories] E --> F[Practice Until Natural]
Step 1: Career Timeline Review ▼

Review your last 3-5 years and list:

  • Projects you worked on
  • Challenges you faced
  • Achievements you're proud of
  • Mistakes and learnings
  • Key relationships and conflicts
  • Times you influenced others
Step 2: Category Brainstorm ▼

For each category, identify 3-5 potential stories:

Category Example Questions
Technical LeadershipDecisions, mentoring, code quality
Conflict ResolutionDisagreements, difficult stakeholders
Failure & LearningMistakes, missed deadlines, feedback
InfluenceLeading without authority, alignment
DeliveryPressure, complex projects, prioritization
GrowthSelf-improvement, learning, weaknesses
Step 3: Selection Criteria ▼

Choose stories that are:

  • Recent: Last 3-5 years
  • Resolved: Complete with outcome
  • Impactful: Staff-level significance
  • Specific: Concrete details available
  • Varied: Different contexts/challenges

The Story Matrix

Create a matrix to track coverage. Each story should cover 2-3 categories:

Story Tech Lead Conflict Failure Influence Delivery Growth
Migration projectXXX
Team disagreementXX
Missed deadlineXX

Goal: 10-15 stories covering all categories with some overlap.

Quantifying Impact

With Metrics

  • "Reduced load time by 40%"
  • "Increased conversion by 15%"
  • "Saved $200K annually"
  • "Reduced incidents from 5/month to 1/quarter"

Without Metrics

  • "Affected 10,000 daily users"
  • "Cut processing time in half"
  • "Received commendation from VP"
  • "Approach adopted by 3 other teams"

Story Versatility

Same Story, Different Angles ▼

Example: Led migration from monolith to microservices

  • Technical Leadership: Focus on architecture analysis, trade-off evaluation
  • Influence: Focus on getting buy-in from reluctant teams
  • Delivery: Focus on planning, risk management, execution
  • Failure: Focus on challenges, what went wrong, recovery

Technique: Keep situation/task similar, emphasize different actions, tailor result to question.

Story Template

## Story Title: [Descriptive Name]

### Categories
Primary: [main category]
Secondary: [additional categories]

### STAR Breakdown

**Situation** (2 sentences max):
[Context and stakes]

**Task** (2 sentences max):
[Your specific role and challenge]

**Action** (4-6 bullet points):
1. First, I...
2. Then, I...
3. Next, I...
4. Finally, I...

**Result** (2-3 sentences):
[Quantified outcome]
[What I learned]
