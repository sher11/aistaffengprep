Situation ~15 seconds

Set the context. Who, what, when, where? Keep it brief but specific enough for the interviewer to understand.

A memorable name for this story (e.g., "Database Migration Crisis")
Which question types can this story answer?
Context: role, team, company scale, timeline, stakes
0 / 200 characters recommended
Questions to answer:
  • What was your role and team?
  • What was the business context?
  • What made this situation challenging or significant?
  • When did this happen? (within last 3-5 years ideally)

Task ~15 seconds

What was YOUR specific responsibility? What were you trying to achieve?

Focus on YOUR role, not the team's goal. Use "I" not "we".
0 / 150 characters recommended
Strong Task statements include:
  • Specific, measurable objectives
  • Your personal accountability (not team's)
  • Constraints you were working within
  • Why YOU were the one to do this

Common mistake: Describing the team's goal instead of your personal responsibility. Interviewers want to know what YOU did.

Action ~60 seconds

This is the meat of your story. What specific steps did YOU take? This is where you demonstrate your skills.

Be specific. Use "I" statements. Include 3-5 concrete actions.
0 / 500+ characters recommended
Actions should demonstrate:
  • Technical skills: What technologies did you use? What was your approach?
  • Leadership: How did you align people? Handle disagreements?
  • Problem-solving: What obstacles did you overcome?
  • Communication: How did you keep stakeholders informed?

Interviewers often ask follow-up questions about your actions. Be prepared to go deeper: "What alternatives did you consider?" "How did you handle resistance?"

Result ~20 seconds

What was the outcome? Quantify the impact whenever possible. Include learnings.

Use numbers! Before/after comparisons are powerful.
0 / 250 characters recommended
Strong Results include:
  • Metrics: Revenue, latency, uptime, efficiency gains
  • Business impact: Customer satisfaction, cost savings
  • Team impact: Processes improved, knowledge shared
  • Personal growth: What you learned, what you'd do differently
